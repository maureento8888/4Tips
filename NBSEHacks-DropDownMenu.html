Mission

Our cause is to find out what current issues reside in Black, particularly, Black female communities hair care (as related to identity) and representation in a predominantly Eurocentric society. Black natural hair vloggers offer empowerment centering around hair care and self-identity in the Black community Provide a way to put forth own narrative and lived experience within a largely Eurocentric-dominant society. (Neil & Mbilishaka, 2019)

Qualitative research in African/Black Diaspora on topic of hair and the meaning of Black hair to Black identity found that among Black women, many don’t meet the recommended physical activity level (150 mins/week) due to issues with hair maintenance and care. Given our knowledge of physical activity’s health benefits, this is an important yet untalked (reword) about topic. (Versey, 2014) “Across African Diaspora, hair is synonymous with identity” - Teteh et al (2019) Due to specific needs for hair care, hair products often contain a lot of endocrine-disrupting chemicals (EDCs)- harms health (may increase breast cancer risk).
